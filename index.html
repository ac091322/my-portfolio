About Me



I've always been drawn to a type of structure—seeking stability and consistency in how I work and the products I produce. Whether it's coding, which I'm excited to pursue as my future career, translating documents during my localization roles from December 2021 to January 2024, or wireframing projects, which I did as a project manager for over seven years, I strive for a clear, organized format that brings coherence to the final result. This focus on structure isn't about following rigid rules, but about ensuring that each project maintains its own internal logic and clarity. After excelling in both localization and project management, I realized that while I was good at these roles, I didn't have true passion for the work.

With advancements in AI tools like ChatGPT, the need for human translators is shrinking, so I knew it was the right time to pivot. Coding naturally appeals to my need to be pixel-perfect—just like in translating, I don't want to be off by a single punctuation mark or type an extra space; whether it's a Word document, Canva logo, or an HTML file, I want the spacing on the right side of my document to be just as precise as the left.

My resolution to make this career change really took hold when I bought the "100 Days of Python" course on Udemy, taught by Angela Yu. In just the first four days of the course, I learned about print statements, strings, variables, and mathematical operators like modulo, which can be used to check for odd and even numbers—a concept I found so fascinating. From these early exercises, I realized just how much fun and interesting coding could be, and it pulled me into this world then and there. After spending too many years in industries where I didn't feel passionate, I discovered coding a bit later than most, but I believe it's not too late!

After completing a software engineering bootcamp, I'm excited to bring my attention to detail and problem-solving skills to a team, contributing to meaningful projects and seeing collaborative efforts come to life. There's something incredibly fulfilling about being part of a group that builds something impactful together.

Outside of work, I love traveling and discovering places I've never been to before. If I get the chance, I'll spend my free time exploring new destinations and capturing the beauty of nature, cityscapes, and architecture with my trusty old phone. Some of the most memorable places I've been to include Hokkaido, Amsterdam, Ibiza, Paris, Bangkok, Taipei, Borocay, and Angkor Wat.

The more I learn, the more I realize just how much I don't know!
